I am a self-proclaimed disaster epidemiologist, generally interested in health impacts of natural hazards. More specifically, I focus on how the measurement of these impacts are affected by the currently utilized methodologies. Additionally, I am interested in business continutity through emergence and improvisation, as well as understanding how overall hazard impact may be moderated through household preparedness and the empowerment of children.

I am currently a dotoral student in epidemiology at the University of Delaware, supervised by Jennifer Horney in the college of Health Sciences.

In 2020, I earned my MPH from Columbia University, where I was mentored by Pam Factor-Litvak. I also hold an undergraduate degree in biomedical engineering from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute.
