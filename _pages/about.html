I am a self-proclaimed disaster epidemiologist. I am currently a dotoral student at the University of Delaware, supervised by in the college of Health Sciences.

I am primarily interested in natural hazards and their associated health impacts, more specifically, I am focused on how the measurement of these impacts are affected by the currently utilized methodologies. Additionally, I am interested in business continutity through improvisation and understanding how overall hazard impact may be moderated through household preparedness and the empowerment of children.

In 2020, I earned my MPH from Columbia University, where I was mentored by Pam Factor-Litvak. I also hold an undergraduate degree in biomedical engineering from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute.

In summer 2019, during my studies, I consulted at the Ghana Health Service, serving in Gushegu, Northern District.
